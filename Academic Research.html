P6

Academic Research

There are two main types of plastics in the oceans and in the garbage islands: the macroplastics and the microplastics. The first ones consist mainly of containers, bags, boxes, lenses and in general complete objects; while the microplastics are those that are derived from the macroplastics, that is to say, they are the small pieces (2 to 5 mm) that sometimes get to get rid of a container.

The big island formed in the Pacific Ocean contains microplastics in general; When the existence of this island was discovered, it was thought that it was only formed by this type of plastic, however, now it exists equally and in an impressive way, macroplastic. This worries experts because over time, these macroplastics can be transformed into microplastics, which makes it much harder to collect. "It's like a time bomb," said Joost Dubois, spokesman for the Ocean Cleanup Foundation. (Bubois,2018).
